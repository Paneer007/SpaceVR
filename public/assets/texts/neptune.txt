
Neptune, the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun, is a cold, windy gas giant with a deep blue coloration. It has a diameter of approximately 49,528 kilometers. Neptune's atmosphere is composed of hydrogen, helium, and traces of methane, giving it a distinct appearance. It has a dynamic system of dark storm systems, including the famous Great Dark Spot. Neptune has a set of rings and 14 known moons. Discovered in 1846, Neptune completes an orbit around the Sun roughly every 165 Earth years.
